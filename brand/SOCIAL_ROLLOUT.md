# KAGE: Social Media Rollout Strategy

## 1. Brand Positioning & Aesthetic
* **Name:** Kage (Japanese for "Shadow")
* **Product:** High-end, browser-based video editor.
* **Aesthetic:** Japanese minimalism, stark contrasts, "silhouette double exposure." Deep blacks, crisp whites, subtle muted accent tones.
* **Tone of Voice:** Intentional, poetic, refined, sparse. We don't yell. We whisper, and people lean in to listen.
* **Core Philosophy:** "The Art of Subtraction." Removing the friction and visual clutter of traditional NLEs (Non-Linear Editors).

## 2. Platform Strategy
* **X (Twitter):** Targeting the indie-maker, tech, and creator economy. Focus on short, poetic text, crisp GIF features, and building in public (with a highly curated, premium veil).
* **Instagram / Threads:** The visual home. Heavy emphasis on our "silhouette double exposure" aesthetic. Carousel posts showing UI details, mood boards, and short, high-fidelity video snippets.
* **LinkedIn:** Targeting agency founders, creative directors, and high-end freelancers. Focus on workflow efficiency, the philosophy of design, and the future of cloud-native post-production.

## 3. Rollout Phases

### Phase 1: The Void (Teaser) - T-Minus 14 Days
* **Objective:** Build intrigue without showing the full product UI.
* **Visuals:** Pure silhouettes. Double exposures of nature (smoke, ink, water) inside the silhouette of a human profile or a cursor.
* **Copy Examples:**
  * *X:* "Most tools add. We subtract. Coming soon."
  * *IG:* A single slow-motion ink drop dissolving into a digital grid. Caption: "Editing, reduced to its essence. Kage."
* **Action:** Launch landing page for early access waitlist. The page is pure black with a white cursor.

### Phase 2: The Form (Feature Reveals) - T-Minus 7 Days
* **Objective:** Show glimpses of the interface. Highlight the browser-native performance.
* **Visuals:** Extreme close-ups of the timeline. The playhead cutting through footage like a katana.
* **Copy Examples:**
  * *LinkedIn:* "Why does a video editor need to look like an airplane cockpit? We rebuilt the timeline from the ground up for the browser. No clutter. Just your footage."
  * *X Thread:* "1/ The modern creator doesn't need 1,000 buttons. They need 10, executed flawlessly. A look at the Kage timeline..."

### Phase 3: The Light (Launch Day) - Day 0
* **Objective:** Full reveal, open the gates, drive sign-ups.
* **Visuals:** A beautiful, 60-second manifesto video. Starting in pure shadow, ending in a stunning double-exposure sequence entirely edited within Kage (in-browser).
* **Copy Examples:**
  * *All Platforms:* "Step out of the dark. Kage is now live. The high-end video editor that lives in your browser. Experience the art of subtraction."
  * *Action:* Product Hunt launch, press releases to design/tech publications.

## 4. Post-Launch: The "Creator as Canvas" Campaign
* Invite top-tier aesthetic creators (filmmakers, architects, designers) to edit a short piece in Kage.
* Feature their timelines and their final exports.
* Create a bespoke "Double Exposure" template in Kage that users can apply to their own footage, driving user-generated content across social platforms.
